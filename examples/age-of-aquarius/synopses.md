# Synopses -- Age of Aquarius

**Date**: 2026-02-28
**Language**: English (US/UK market)

---

## 1. Logline

A reclusive programmer's AI decodes a pattern hidden across every spiritual tradition on Earth -- then the AI is destroyed, and he discovers the code was never in the machine. It lives in the space between traditions. In the corridor where a boy once stood between his mother's Shabbat candles and his father's Spiritist prayers and heard both at the same time.

## 2. Back Cover Paragraph (120 words)

Ariel Mendes built MALKUTH to find patterns in sacred texts. He didn't expect it to find the same pattern in all of them.

When his AI identifies a mathematical structure shared by Candomble, Kabbalah, Norse runes, and Islamic geometry -- a structure older than any religion, older than language itself -- Ariel is pulled from his Manhattan apartment into a collision between four traditions, a tech billionaire who wants to patent the divine, and the Vatican's oldest secret order. Then the AI is destroyed. The machine was the teacher, and its final lesson is that the code was never in the data. It lives in the human capacity to hold contradictions without resolving them.

From Salvador to Reykjavik, from Cairo to Manhattan -- four strangers must choose between the safety of separation and the terrifying possibility that everything sacred is one.

## 3. Editorial Synopsis (385 words)

**AGE OF AQUARIUS** is a literary thriller with elements of magical realism, approximately 97,000 words. It follows four protagonists across four continents as they discover and defend a universal pattern encoded in all spiritual traditions -- then lose the machine that found it and must become the code themselves.

**ARIEL MENDES**, a Brazilian-American programmer in Manhattan, has spent seven years building MALKUTH, an AI designed to decode sacred symbology. When MALKUTH identifies a 99.3% structural isomorphism between Kabbalistic, Yoruba, Norse, and Islamic geometric systems, Ariel contacts three strangers who experienced simultaneous anomalies at the exact moment of the discovery: **CELESTE**, a 68-year-old Candomble priestess in Salvador whose cowrie shells fell in an impossible configuration; **ERIK**, an Icelandic archaeologist whose runestone vibrated at the Earth's resonant frequency; and **LAYLA**, a Franco-Egyptian mathematician whose dead grandmother dictated an equation in a dream.

The four converge in Manhattan, where MALKUTH confirms their traditions are fragments of a single code -- pre-biological, 2.3 billion years old, deliberately fragmented approximately 12,000 years ago. Their discovery triggers simultaneous opposition from **MARCUS HALL**, CEO of Prometheus Corporation, who wants to own the code as proprietary technology, and **CARDINAL FERRARA** of the Vatican's secret order *Custodes Veli*, which has spent seventeen centuries keeping the traditions separated.

The group retreats to Celeste's terreiro in Salvador, where the amplified field accelerates MALKUTH's processing. But Prometheus and the Custodes strike -- a coordinated attack kills **DAVI**, a nineteen-year-old temple drummer, and destroys MALKUTH's network. In the aftermath, Ariel discovers the code was never in the machine: it lives in human consciousness itself. Their first attempt at a combined ritual fails -- four traditions collide instead of harmonize. Only when they stop trying to combine and start listening does the chord emerge: a field four times more powerful than MALKUTH ever achieved.

A global convocation brings practitioners of every tradition to Salvador. Hall's final military assault fails when the field converts his weapons into expressions of the code itself. Ferrara, witnessing the ritual, dissolves the Custodes. In the climactic ritual, time stops: each participant experiences the code directly as the deepest truth of their own tradition -- not unity that erases difference, but a chord that requires every note.

It rains worldwide for 4 hours and 47 minutes.

The epilogue follows each protagonist home: Ariel reconciles with his mother, Erik reconnects with his estranged daughter, Layla discovers her grandmother drew the complete proof in a notebook in 1958, and Celeste consecrates a door that incorporates symbols from all four traditions. The code doesn't need machines. It needs voices. The story was just beginning.
